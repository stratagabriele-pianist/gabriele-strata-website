Praised for the clarity and depth of his interpretations, Gabriele Strata is distinguished by an
exceptionally personal and reflective approach to the piano repertoire, often shaped through
thoughtfully curated programs.
He drew international attention at the 2025 Fryderyk Chopin Competition in Warsaw, where
audiences and critics alike were captivated by his artistry, and was subsequently praised by
Gramophone for the strength of his musical personality and the clarity of his interpretative
vision. Shortly afterward, he stepped in at the last minute for Maria João Pires in Mozart’s Piano
Concerto No. 27 under Kent Nagano, a performance that received critical acclaim and marked a
turning point in his career.

A prizewinner at several international competitions including the Concours International de
musique de Montréal, the Rio de Janeiro International Piano Competition, and Premio Venezia,
he was appointed in 2025 artist-in-residence for three years by Amici della Musica di Firenze,
performing regularly in recital and chamber music at the Teatro della Pergola. He also appears
internationally across Europe, the Americas, and Asia at major venues including the Berliner
Philharmonie, Wigmore Hall and Barbican Centre in London, Amsterdam’s Concertgebouw,
Teatro La Fenice in Venice, Sala Verdi in Milano, Maison Symphonique in Montréal, and
Yokohama’s Minato Mirai Hall.

His repertoire spans from Baroque to contemporary music, reflecting a broad and curious
musical outlook. He has a particular affinity for curating recital programs that place works in
dialogue, revealing unexpected connections and offering fresh perspectives on the piano
repertoire.

He studied with Boris Berman, Benedetto Lupo, and Julia Mustonen-Dahlkvist, earning a Master
of Music from Yale University at 19 and graduating with highest honors from the Accademia
Nazionale di Santa Cecilia. He has been awarded the Medal of the President of the Italian
Republic in recognition of his outstanding artistic achievements.
